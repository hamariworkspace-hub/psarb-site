About us

PSARB is the national space regulatory authority mandated to oversee outer space and upper-atmosphere activities, implementing the Space Activities Rules 2024 in coordination with national institutions.

Space legislation

Space Activities Rules provide the umbrella framework. Sector-specific regulations govern Satcom landing rights, Earth Observation, PNT services and Space Transportation authorizations.

Registration

Registration with PSARB requires prior incorporation with SECP (where applicable). Operators create an online profile and then submit sector-specific applications.

Regulatory sectors

PSARB regulates four core domains. Each sector has specific eligibility criteria, application requirements and compliance obligations.

Satellite Communications (Satcom)

Landing rights and registration for communication payloads providing services into Pakistan, including FSS, BSS and broadband constellations.

Focus: landing rights, spectrum coordination, quality of service.

Earth Observation (EO)

Licensing of EO missions and data services that acquire, process or distribute imagery and data relating to Pakistan.

Focus: data policy, security controls, downstream services.

Positioning, Navigation & Timing (PNT)

Authorization for GNSS augmentation and PNT services supporting critical infrastructure, safety and commercial applications.

Focus: integrity, continuity, resilience.

Space Transportation

Licensing of launch, re-entry and related space transportation activities with a nexus to Pakistan, including payloads and launch services.

Focus: safety, risk management, insurance and international obligations.

About PSARB

PSARB functions as the principal regulatory body for outer space and upper-atmosphere activities with a Pakistani nexus, supporting national security, technological development and commercial use of space.

PSARB is responsible for:

  • Authorizing and supervising space activities involving Pakistan, including satellite operations and launch activities.
  • Issuing registrations, authorizations and related decisions for operators and systems.
  • Ensuring compliance with national law, international commitments and technical standards.
  • Coordinating with national bodies responsible for spectrum, telecommunications, broadcasting and security.
  • Supporting a predictable and transparent regulatory environment for investment in Pakistan‚Äôs space economy.
